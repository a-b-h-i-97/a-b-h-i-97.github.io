My Experiences
Computer Science & Engineering Association, NITC
Secretary
2018 - 2019 | Kozhikode, India
I had been an executive of CSEA since 2016 and had been actively involved in all of its activities. As secretary, I led the organization and oversaw all its regular activities for the academic year. During my tenure, we started Interview Diaries - an internal repository of interview experiences exclusively for the students of NITC. I conceptualized and oversaw the transition of our department newsletter, Threads onto a new platform with emphasis on usability and maintainability. Apart from regular talks and workshops, we organized an internal hackathon for NITC students. I was also involved with FOSSMeet and led the talks team at the 2019 edition of the event.

CSEA showed me the power of teamwork - what a focused group of individuals with a goal can achieve. I realized that it takes people of quality to take initiatives that transform good to the best. It improved my leadership qualities, made me a better listener and taught me to respect others peoples' choices and opinion. It taught me that honest and critical feedback is one of the strongest tools to keep improving as an individual and as a unit.
Microsoft Teams
Software Development Intern
Summer 2018 | Bengaluru, India
I worked with the Calendar team of Microsoft Teams. I developed reusable UI components using React and Typescript to support the recurrence feature in the new events workflow. Apart from learning React and Typescript, I got hands on experience with UI libraries like Semantic UI, testing frameworks like Jest and UI tools like Storybooks. I also realized the importance of things like accessibility and localization which come into play when developing software at a global scale.

Microsoft was my first corporate job, developing software for a global user base using a highly structured software engineering workflow collaborating with developers around the globe. I got to work with an amazing bunch of people who were always ready to help each other. Most importantly it taught me that taking ownership is beyond taking responsibility. The offer for a permanent role at Microsoft at the end of the internship was the icing on the cake.
Fourth Frontier
Summer Intern
Summer 2017 | Bengaluru, India
The summer internship at Fourth Frontier was my first real developer job. I worked on developing algorithms for processing biological signals like ECG in a highly memory efficient manner. These algorithms were implemented in C to run natively on a device that was being built. I did some minor work on their Android application as well which involved updating certain algorithms running within the app as they evolved.

It was an amazing experience to work with a small group of people who were incredibly passionate about the work that they did. The best part of the experience was to see the product evolve in all aspects. There were electronics engineers working on the hardware, designers who were working on its aesthetics and comfort, developers working on algorithms, mobile applications, web frameworks and people who were working on marketing and other business aspects. All of this happened in a space where all of us could see each other.
National Institute of Technology, Calicut
B.Tech, Computer Science & Engineering
2015 - 2019 | Kozhikode, India
The four years at NITC was a truly transformative educational experience. Courses offered by awesome professors there gave me sound fundamental knowledge about the discipline and helped me develop the passion to keep learning everyday. Moreover they taught me to put knowledge before grades and integrity over everything else. I met so many people, each talented in their own ways. I made friendships and memories for a lifetime and learned to respect them. The diversity there taught me to acknowledge my privileges. Overall, it made me a better person and taught me to be professional in every aspect.
